☀️ The Sun

  • The Sun is a giant star at the centre of our solar system.
  • It gives Earth heat and light.
  • The Sun is so big that one million Earths could fit inside it.
  • It is about 150 million kilometres from Earth.
  • The surface of the Sun is called the photosphere.

🌕 The Moon

  • The Moon orbits Earth every 27 days.
  • It is covered in craters, mountains and dust.
  • We can see the Moon because sunlight reflects off it.
  • The Moon has different phases: new moon, crescent, half and full moon.
  • There is no air or water on the Moon.

🪐 Planets

  • There are eight planets in our solar system.
  • All planets orbit the Sun.
  • The four inner planets are rocky: Mercury, Venus, Earth and Mars.
  • The four outer planets are gas giants: Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus and Neptune.
  • Saturn has beautiful rings made of ice and rock.

⭐ Stars

  • Stars are giant balls of hot, glowing gas.
  • They make their own light and heat.
  • Our Sun is the closest star to Earth.
  • Stars come in different colours: blue, white, yellow and red.
  • Groups of stars are called constellations.

☄️ Comets

  • Comets are made of ice, dust and rock.
  • They travel through space in a long oval orbit.
  • As they get near the Sun, they form a bright glowing tail.
  • The tail can be millions of kilometres long.
  • Halley's Comet visits our solar system every 75 years.

🌀 Galaxies

  • A galaxy is a huge collection of millions of stars.
  • Our galaxy is called the Milky Way.
  • Galaxies can be spiral, elliptical or irregular in shape.
  • There are billions of galaxies in the universe.
  • The Milky Way is so wide it takes light 100,000 years to cross it.

✏️ Simple & Compound Sentences
Simple
What is it?

One complete idea. One subject + one action.

Examples
The Moon is rocky.
The Sun shines brightly.
Comets have icy tails.
Compound
What is it?

Two ideas joined by a conjunction.

Examples
The Moon is rocky and it has craters.
Stars are far away but we can see them.
Comets melt so they form a tail.
🔗 Conjunctions to use
and but so or because
⚡ How to build a compound sentence
Simple sentence 1 + conjunction + Simple sentence 2

Examples:

The Sun is a star and it gives us light.

The Moon is rocky but it has no air.

🔵 Noun Groups
What is a noun?

A noun is a person, place or thing.

MoonstarplanetcometcratertailSunorbit
What is a noun group?

A noun plus describing words to add detail.

noun → adjective + noun
star → a brilliant, distant star
⚡ Basic → Better
Basic sentence
The Moon has craters.
↓ add a noun group ↓
Better sentence
The rocky, grey Moon has deep craters.
Basic sentence
The Sun gives us light.
↓ add a noun group ↓
Better sentence
The blazing, enormous Sun gives us light.

🟡 Verb Groups
What is a verb?

A verb is an action or doing word.

orbitsshinesburnsspinsglowstravels
What is a verb group?

A verb plus extra words to describe how, when or where.

verb → verb + how/when/where
shines → shines brilliantly every day
⚡ Basic → Better
Basic sentence
The Earth moves.
↓ add a verb group ↓
Better sentence
The Earth spins slowly and travels around the Sun.
Basic sentence
A comet goes through space.
↓ add a verb group ↓
Better sentence
A comet hurtles through space at incredible speed.

📋 Steps to write your paragraph
1
Think
What do you know about your celestial object? Use the Celestial Objects tab for ideas.
2
Build noun groups
Choose your nouns and add describing words. Example: the enormous, blazing Sun.
3
Build verb groups
Choose your verbs and add words to show how. Example: shines brilliantly every day.
4
Write 3 to 5 sentences
Mix simple and compound sentences. Use different sentence starters.
5
Read it back
Does it make sense? Is it interesting? Then move on to editing!
✨ Example paragraph about the Moon
The rocky, grey Moon orbits Earth slowly every month. It is covered in deep craters and it has no air or water. The cold, dusty surface reflects sunlight from the Sun. We can see the Moon at night because it glows with reflected light. The Moon rises and sets in the sky just like the Sun.
